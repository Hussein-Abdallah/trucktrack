01 — Overview

## Who Are We Comparing Against?

TruckTrack

Ottawa, Canada · 2026

OUR PRODUCT

Hyper-local Ottawa-first platform combining real-time scheduling, digital loyalty, catering, Instagram sync, bilingual (EN/FR), and winter pause. B2B SaaS subscription model. Built by a solo developer who lives in the market.

Roaming Hunger

Los Angeles, USA · Founded 2009

MARKET LEADER

The largest food truck platform in North America. 18,500+ trucks, 10M+ meals facilitated. Primarily a catering marketplace for corporate and event clients. Software tools free for vendors — revenue from service fees charged to event hosts.

StreetFoodFinder

Los Angeles, USA · Founded 2012

CONSUMER APP

Consumer-first food truck finder app. Select US cities only (Ohio, Raleigh, Phoenix, Nashville, Tampa). 150K+ downloads. Free for both consumers and operators. Solo developer, limited resources, sporadic updates.

Truckster

Colorado, USA · Founded ~2018

FULL STACK

Full-featured two-sided platform: consumer finder + vendor dashboard with mobile ordering, analytics, catering leads. Operating in Colorado, Austin, Portland, Miami, Kansas City. Vendor subscription model + commission on orders.

Street Food App

Multiple Cities · Founded 2011

PRESENT IN OTTAWA

Global food truck finder active in Ottawa since 2011. Effectively abandoned — only 3 trucks listed in Ottawa despite 78+ operating. No loyalty, no notifications, no operator tools. Direct incumbent to displace.

06 — Real User Feedback

## What People Actually Say

Verbatim themes from App Store, Google Play, and Yelp reviews. These are the patterns — not one-off opinions.

Roaming Hunger ✓ Positives

- Far and away the easiest booking experience for a food truck event I've done to date.
- They helped me grow my catering business by 25%. I love working with them.
- The 120% money-back guarantee gave us total peace of mind for our corporate lunch.
- Booking a truck for our office took 10 minutes. They handled everything.

Roaming Hunger ✗ Negatives

- The consumer app feels like an afterthought — it's clearly built for corporate bookings, not everyday use.
- Hard to know if trucks are actually open right now vs. generally operating in my area.
- No push notifications when a favourite truck updates their location. I have to keep checking.
- The app pulls from social media but the data is often hours out of date.

StreetFoodFinder ✓ Positives

- Shows trucks broken down by open now, today, and upcoming days — love that view.
- Found several trucks I didn't know existed within a mile of my house.
- Developer is receptive to feedback and actually updates the app based on suggestions.
- Discovered food truck dining during the pandemic using this — still use it weekly.

StreetFoodFinder ✗ Negatives

- Went to find a truck that was listed as open — it wasn't there. First time using it, already frustrated.
- The map shows every truck ever regardless of date — mass of useless pins until I find the filter.
- Nearly 2 years without an update. Great idea but developers need to commit or quit.
- App can't connect to SFF servers — completely unusable for days. No update from the developer.
- Search function is useless if I want a specific cuisine or area — too basic.

Truckster ✓ Positives

- Amazing app — has schedule including brewery info, events, AND their menu all in one place.
- Truckster helped us book catering for a party of 80. The team was incredibly responsive.
- Finally an app where I can find food trucks AND skip the line with mobile ordering.
- The vendor app helped us manage orders during our busiest shifts — staff could keep up.

Truckster ✗ Negatives

- With less than 50K consumer downloads and under 1K vendor downloads, charging $15/month is a stretch — the value isn't there yet in my city.
- App tried to charge $15/month but there are almost no trucks listed in my area. Why would I pay?
- Portland is completely useless — missing over a dozen permanent food cart venues that have existed for years.
- App crashes when no trucks are found nearby — flashing screen could trigger seizures.
- Good platform concept but city coverage is too thin outside of Colorado.

Street Food App (Ottawa) ✗ Ottawa-specific

- Only shows the same three trucks every time, regardless of where I am in Ottawa.
- I see open food trucks everywhere but the app shows nothing near me. Useless.
- Not sure who is responsible for updating trucks — feels like nobody is.
- This could be a great app but it clearly hasn't been maintained. Ottawa deserves better.

TruckTrack → Our Response

- Inaccurate data → Daily 8am operator reminder solves this. Approval layer ensures quality.
- No push notifications → Core feature from Day 1. Per-truck granular controls.
- Too expensive for thin coverage → Free tier with clear upgrade value. $19 only once we've proven the map.
- City coverage gaps → Ottawa-first density strategy avoids thin coverage at launch.
- Consumer app feels secondary → Both sides designed equally — not catering-first.

08 — Strategic Opportunities

## Gaps Confirmed by Competitor Failures

Every negative review above is a product requirement for TruckTrack. Here's what the market has validated as genuinely unsolved.

📍

#### Accurate Real-Time Data

Every competitor's #1 complaint is inaccurate location data. Trucks don't update. Consumers show up to empty spots. TruckTrack's daily reminder system + approval quality gate directly addresses this — nobody else has.

Source: StreetFoodFinder, Street Food App, Truckster reviews

🇫🇷

#### French Language — Untouched

Zero competitors offer French. Ottawa's 33% francophone population and the entire Gatineau market are completely unserved. A bilingual app is a genuine first-mover advantage that no US-based competitor will prioritize.

Source: Market gap — no competitor offers this

❄️

#### Seasonal Pause Nobody Built

Truckster operators in northern cities complain about paying when trucks are closed for winter. Not one platform has solved this. TruckTrack's zero-charge pause with spring reactivation notification is a direct response to real churn.

Source: Truckster Play Store reviews

🎟️

#### Loyalty Is Completely Missing

Not a single food truck app offers digital loyalty. Every truck still hands out paper stamp cards that get lost. This is an entirely open feature category that TruckTrack owns exclusively.

Source: Feature gap across all competitors

💳

#### Subscription Needs to Justify Itself

Truckster's biggest complaint: operators won't pay $15/mo if consumer density is thin. TruckTrack's free tier builds the consumer base first, then charges operators only once the value is proven. Sequence matters.

Source: Truckster Play Store — "50K consumer downloads before charging"

🎪

#### Festival Mode Nobody Thought Of

Every competitor misses festivals as both a revenue event and acquisition spike. At Ottawa's annual rally 10,000+ attendees are the perfect captive audience. No platform has built a dedicated festival experience.

Source: Market gap — validated by Ottawa festival scale

**The clearest lesson from every competitor:** they all tried to scale nationally before they had density locally. Truckster in Portland is useless. StreetFoodFinder in Houston shows no trucks. Roaming Hunger in Ottawa has 78 trucks but only surfaces them for catering. TruckTrack wins by going deep in one city before going wide — the exact opposite playbook of every competitor that failed.
